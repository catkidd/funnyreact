NEPAL

Best places to visit



Swayambhunath, also known as the Monkey Temple, is an ancient Buddhist temple complex located in Kathmandu, Nepal. The temple is considered to be one of the most sacred Buddhist sites in Nepal, and is also a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
The origins of the temple are said to date back to the 5th century, although it is likely that the complex was built on an even older sacred site. According to legend, the temple was built by the monkey king, Manjushree, who is said to have used his divine powers to cut a passage through a nearby hill, revealing the natural pond that still exists within the temple complex. The temple was later expanded and renovated by various rulers throughout the centuries, including the Licchavi kings of the 4th century and the Malla kings of the 14th century.
Swayambhunath is also known for its striking architecture, which is a blend of traditional Nepalese and Tibetan styles. The temple complex is made up of several temples, shrines, and statues, including the main stupa, which is a large, dome-shaped structure that is covered in gold and painted with the eyes of the Buddha. The temple also features intricate carvings, colorful prayer flags, and other decorative elements that make it a truly unique and beautiful place to visit.
The temple complex is also home to a large population of monkeys, which is where it gets its nickname "Monkey Temple." These monkeys are considered sacred and are protected by the temple authorities. Visitors can feed and observe the monkeys, but they should be cautious as they can be quite aggressive and should be avoided when they are hungry.
Swayambhunath is one of the most important tourist attractions in Nepal, attracting thousands of visitors each year. The temple is considered to be one of the best places to gain an understanding of Buddhism and the culture of Nepal, and it is also a popular spot for taking photographs, especially at sunset when the temple is bathed in a beautiful golden light.
